Privacy notice for {{ productName }}

This notice sets out how we will use your personal data, and your rights. It is made under Articles 13 and/or 14 of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR).

Your data

Purpose

The purposes for which we are processing your personal data are:

  • Enabling login to the platform
  • Chat history and log information will be pseudonymised and analysis of this will be used to improve the service for you as the user.
  • Processing any personal data you choose to upload to the system as part of your work and in accordance with the guidance

The data

We will process the following personal data:

  • Full name
  • Organisational email address
  • Chat history

Legal basis of processing

The legal basis for processing your personal data is:

  • To perform a task in the public interest (Article 6(1)(e) of the UK GDPR and section 8 of the Data Protection Act 2018

Recipients

Your personal data will be made available to {{ productName }} user researchers and with analysts/researchers within the Department for Business and Trade for analysis to improve the service, you can opt out of this by emailing: {{ contact_email }}

As your personal data will be stored on our IT infrastructure it will also be shared with our data processors who provide email and document management and storage services.

  • Each user's content is stored in isolated personal S3 buckets
  • Access controls ensure only you can access your content and generations
  • SOC monitoring is in place to detect and prevent potential data breaches
  • In the future, DataDog LLM observability features may be implemented for enhanced monitoring for security purposes

Retention

Any personal data you upload as part of documents or chats will be automatically deleted after 6 months.

Personal Data Usage Guidelines

When working with personal data, the use cases which {{ productName }} supports:

  1. Consultations processing and analysis
  2. Survey response processing and analysis
  3. Correspondence drafting

When using {{ productName }} to process personal data as part of your work:

  • Where possible, remove or redact personal data before uploading if not essential for your work
  • Do not process data related to children or vulnerable individuals
  • Avoid uploading special category data (health data, racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, data relating to sex life or sexual orientation) unless necessary for your specific role
  • Remember you remain responsible for complying with data protection principles when processing others' personal data outlined in the data protection policy

Your rights

You have the right to request information about how your personal data are processed, and to request a copy of that personal data.

You have the right to request that any inaccuracies in your personal data are rectified without delay, however, technical restrictions mean that personal data cannot be directly amended once entered (permitted under Article 19 UK GDPR).

You have the right to request that any incomplete personal data are completed, including by means of a supplementary statement, however, technical restrictions mean that personal data cannot be directly amended once entered (permitted under Article 19 UK GDPR).

You have the right to request that your personal data are erased if there is no longer a justification for them to be processed.

You have the right in certain circumstances (for example, where accuracy is contested) to request that the processing of your personal data is restricted.

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data where it is processed for direct marketing purposes.

You have the right to withdraw consent to the processing of your personal data at any time.

International transfers

As your personal data is stored on our IT infrastructure, and shared with our data processors, it may be transferred and stored securely outside the UK, in EU West 1 (Ireland) on DBT Infrastructure. There is a reciprocal Adequacy decision between the UK and Ireland, personal data will not be stored outside the EU.

Complaints

If you consider that your personal data has been misused or mishandled, you may make a complaint to the Information Commissioner, who is an independent regulator. The Information Commissioner can be contacted at: Information Commissioner's Office, Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire, SK9 5AF, or 0303 123 1113, or icocasework@ico.org.uk. Any complaint to the Information Commissioner is without prejudice to your right to seek redress through the courts.

Contact details

The data controller for your personal data is the {{ departmental_owner }}. The contact details for the data controller are: {{ department_address | safe }}.

The contact details for the data controller's Data Protection Officer are: data.protection@businessandtrade.gov.uk .

The Data Protection Officer provides independent advice and monitoring of {{ departmental_owner }}'s use of personal information.

Changes to this notice

We may change this privacy notice. When we make changes to this notice, the "last updated" date at the bottom of this page will also change. Any changes to this privacy notice will apply to you and your data immediately. If these changes affect how your personal data is processed, {{ departmental_owner }} will take reasonable steps to make sure you know.
