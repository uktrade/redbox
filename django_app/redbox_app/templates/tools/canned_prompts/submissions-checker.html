The {{ tool.name }} acts as a 'health check' for ministerial submission drafts.

Your submission is reviewed against 7 criteria. For each criterion, you'll receive a score along with a short explanation and specific examples. Once all 7 parts are checked, you'll get an average score and summary.

To use the Submissions Checker, upload your draft submission document and click the send icon.

How your submission is scored and reviewed

The tool evaluates your submission against 7 key criteria and rates each one from 1 (poor) to 5 (excellent).

The 7 criteria are:

  • plain English: encourages simple language, minimises jargon and ensures acronyms are explained
  • clear recommendations: checks that recommendations are explicit and logically justified
  • context provision: confirms background information and rationale are clear
  • evidence support: verifies assertions are backed by facts and data with transparent sources
  • options analysis: examines whether multiple options are presented and compared
  • trade-offs articulation: assesses how competing considerations and risks are balanced
  • narrative flow: supports smooth, logical movement of ideas for easy understanding.
How to get more from this tool

You can get more from this tool by:

  • uploading any relevant contextual documents to the submission’s topic. Use the message box to ask Assist to consider them in its review
  • asking Assist to only consider specific criteria. For example, as you update your draft you can ask it to review it only for narrative flow
